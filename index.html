Design of High-Speed 8-bit Vedic Multiplier using Brent-Kung Adders

Aruru Sai Kumar, U. Siddhesh, N. Sai Kiran, K. Bhavitha

DOI: 10.1109/ICCCNT54827.2022.9984591
IEEE Xplore Repository

Abstract

This work proposes an optimized 8-bit multiplier architecture that integrates Vedic multiplication principles with Brent-Kung parallel prefix adders. By utilizing the Urdhva Tiryagbhyam sutra for parallel partial-product generation and Brent-Kung logic for efficient carry handling, the design achieves a significant reduction in propagation delay. Experimental results show that the proposed Vedic + Brent-Kung multiplier outperforms conventional architectures like Wallace Tree and Dadda multipliers in terms of speed and logic depth. This paper details the architectural hierarchy and provides a comparative performance analysis.

1. Introduction

Multiplication is a fundamental operation in digital signal processing (DSP) and high-performance computing. Conventional multiplier architectures often suffer from high propagation delays due to carry propagation in the final addition stages. This research explores the synergy between the ancient Vedic multiplication technique and modern fast prefix addition to create a high-speed arithmetic unit.

2. Proposed Architecture

The 8-bit multiplier is designed hierarchically, starting from 2-bit Vedic units. The top-level 8-bit unit instantiates four 4-bit Vedic blocks, whose intermediate products are aligned and merged using a high-speed carry network.

2.1 Brent-Kung Prefix Addition

To further reduce the critical path, the final addition stage employs the Brent-Kung prefix tree. This reduces carry depth, allowing for faster carry resolution across the 16-bit product width.
